Editorial Policy

This editorial policy explains how the site maintains topical focus, how pages are reviewed, and why careful wording matters in a niche that often suffers from inflated claims.

Topical scope

The site publishes only within one connected topic cluster so pages reinforce each other instead of drifting into unrelated subjects.

The core scope includes free tablet searches, Lifeline clarity, provider comparison, document preparation, and related application questions. The site avoids generic publishing outside that cluster.

Accuracy standard

Pages are written to reflect official program reality as closely as possible and to separate public support rules from provider-side offers.

That means the site avoids broad guarantees that cannot be supported. It also means the writing acknowledges where provider terms and inventory can vary by company or location.

Update process

Pages are reviewed and revised when key eligibility language, program status, or application details need to be refreshed.

Where the topic touches official consumer information, the site keeps those source-of-truth pages visible and avoids replacing them with unsupported claims.

Writing style

Articles are written in plain language, with direct answers near headings and supporting detail underneath.

This keeps the pages more useful for readers and also helps the site maintain a clean semantic structure.
